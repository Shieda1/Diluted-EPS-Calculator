What is the diluted EPS?
The diluted earnings per share (EPS) is a measurement of the residual net profits that is distributable to each share for the total common equity outstanding.
How to calculate diluted EPS?
To calculate diluted EPS you should use the following formula:
diluted EPS = (net income - preferred dividends) / (outstanding shares + diluted shares)
Example: Company x has $13,000,000 in net income, $3,000,000 in preferred dividends, $21,000,000 in outstanding shares, $6,400,000 of employee stock options and $17,300,000 worth of convertable bonds, calculate the diluted EPS.
First we have to calculate the diluted shares:
diluted shares = $6,400,000 + $17,300,000
diluted shares = $23,700,000
Now let's calculate the diluted EPS:
diluted EPS = (net income - preferred dividends) / (outstanding shares + diluted shares)
diluted EPS = ($13,000,000 - $3,000,000) / ($21,000,000 + $23,700,000)
diluted EPS = $0.35 per share
Why use diluted EPS?
The diluted EPS is used to provide a more comprehensive view of potential per share profitability because alot of firms have extra shares in reserve.
